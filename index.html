Here's the Deal

10 weeks. Housing and relocation assistance. Working with industry pros. Attending executive-led speaker sessions. Serving communities. You get it all with a Walmart internship. Each summer, we select about 300 of the best of the best to take a look under the hood of the world’s largest retailer at the Walmart Home Office in Bentonville, Arkansas and Walmart Global eCommerce in San Bruno, California. These are our #WMTInterns.

#WMTInterns get to own an impactful project that’s make or break for the business in a ton of career areas:

Engineering

Technical problem solver? Number lover? Systems thinker-er? Engineers find homes in Logistics, Store Operations, Innovation,

Real Estate and HR businesses at Walmart, to name a few.

Marketing

Our marketing team influences every product, in every retail unit, across all of the regions Wal-Mart Stores Inc. operates. It’s a HUGE responsibility for

every person on the team, (even interns!)

Finance

There’s a reason this box is green! If you’re interested in Accounting, Tax or Business Strategy, we have a seat for you managing the money for

the Fortune 1 corporation.

eCommerce

eComm and its focus on mobile/web is the fastest-growing area in retail. If a fast, fun, BIG idea environment is your thing, eComm is a great

place to make a huge impact.

Merchandising & Replinshment

Control freaks wanted: Merchandising and Replenishment decides what items get on the shelves,

where they’re placed in stores and make sure those shelves stay stocked. Not a bad gig.

Retail Operations

Each of our retail outlets is a multimillion-dollar retail operation. If you think of yourself as a process-oriented leader, business and financial

guru, and communicator (piece of cake, right?), Retail Operations might be your style.

Technology

Walmart’s tech teams do it BIG, whether we’re talking about data, information security or the fact that together, they make up the

biggest division of the world’s largest corporate workforce.

Sounds Cool, Right? Here Are The Details

Who can apply: If you’re motivated and looking for a way to stand above the crowd when you graduate, you’re just what Walmart is looking for. To vie for a spot as a #WMTIntern, you must currently be enrolled in an academic program at an accredited US college or university. Read on to take a look at what our current interns and first-year employees have to say.

When you can apply: Undergraduate internships are filled during the fall semester (August to December) preceding your summer internship class. Additionally, Graduate and PhD internships are filled during the early spring semester (January to March) preceding your internship.

FAQ’s

Q: Are Walmart internships paid?
A: Yes! #WMTInterns earn a boatload of experience and hey, it's paid.

Q: I may want an internship in a field outside of my major. Can I do that?
A: Sure, if you have the right experience, but it will probably be most beneficial if it aligns with your major.

Q: Can I complete my internship in my college town/hometown?
A: Corporate interns complete their internship at the Walmart Home Office in Bentonville, Arkansas or Walmart Global eCommerce in San Bruno, California. We do offer several other internships in field offices, distribution centers, and stores. Check out our Walmart Field Internships to learn more.

How to apply

Walmart’s Campus recruiters visit schools all over the U.S. and rides a circuit of national collegiate organizations throughout the year. Check out our upcoming events and give us a shout when we’re on your campus. If we’re not heading to your school soon, reach out to your career center and tell them you want to be a #WMTIntern. They’ll know what to do.
